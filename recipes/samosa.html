How to Make Beef Samosa

Description

Beef samosas are a popular East African snack, known for their crispy exterior and flavorful filling. These triangular pastries are filled with spiced ground beef, onions, and herbs, creating a delicious blend of textures and tastes. Samosas are commonly enjoyed as an appetizer or snack and are often served with a side of chutney or hot sauce.

In East Africa, beef samosas are a favorite during special occasions, tea time, and street food culture. The filling is encased in a thin pastry dough, which is then folded into the signature triangular shape and deep-fried to golden perfection. The result is a savory treat that is both crunchy and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • Ground Beef - 500g
  • Onion (finely chopped) - 1 medium
  • Garlic (minced) - 3 cloves
  • Ginger (minced) - 1 teaspoon
  • Green Chilies (optional, finely chopped) - 1-2
  • Fresh Coriander (chopped) - a handful
  • Ground Cumin - 1 teaspoon
  • Ground Coriander - 1 teaspoon
  • Garam Masala - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt - to taste
  • Pastry Dough Sheets (store-bought or homemade) - 10-12
  • Vegetable Oil - for frying

Steps

  1. In a pan, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies (if using). Cook for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the ground beef to the pan, breaking it up as it cooks. Stir in the ground cumin, ground coriander, garam masala, and salt.
  4. Cook the beef until it is fully browned and cooked through. Stir in the chopped fresh coriander and remove the pan from heat.
  5. Let the beef filling cool slightly. Meanwhile, prepare your pastry dough sheets.
  6. Cut the pastry sheets into strips, about 3 inches wide. Place a spoonful of the beef filling at one end of each strip.
  7. Fold the pastry over the filling to form a triangle, and continue folding until the entire strip is used. Seal the edges with a little water.
  8. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the samosas in batches until they are golden brown and crispy.
  9. Drain the fried samosas on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  10. Serve the beef samosas hot, with your favorite chutney or hot sauce.

Enjoy your crispy and flavorful Beef Samosas!
